About

I am a researcher at the UIB Meteorology Group, working on mesoscale meteorology and high-impact weather in the Western Mediterranean — from meteotsunami prediction and severe convective environments to, more recently, rare snowfall events over complex terrain. My work combines numerical modelling (MM5, WRF, TRAM, CM1) with statistical and machine-learning approaches. I spent several years leading R&D at Meteoclim Services, a UIB spin-off, supported by a Torres Quevedo fellowship, applying climate science to real-world problems in tourism, energy and water management — an experience that still shapes how I work. I currently teach in the Physics Department and hold an Assistant Professor (PAD) position.
